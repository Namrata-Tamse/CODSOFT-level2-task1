A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam (A.P.J. Abdul Kalam) was an eminent scientist and the 11th President of India.He was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India, and passed away on July 27, 2015. Abdul Kalamwas widely known as the "Missile Man of India" for his contributions to India's missile and space technology programs.

About The Legend

👉🏾 A. P. J. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was born ob October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India

👉🏾He served as the 11th President of India from 2002 to 2007

👉🏾Kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the Madras Institute of Technology and in 1958 joined joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO)

👉🏾In 1969 , he moved to the Indian Space Research Organisation, where he was project director of the SLV-|||, the first satellite lauch vehicle that was both designed and produced in India.

👉🏾Rejoining DRRO in 1982, Kalam planned the program that produced a number of successful missiles, which helped ean him the nickname "Missile Man"

👉🏾Among those successful was Agni, Indias first intermediate-range ballistic missile, which incorporated aspects of the SLV-||| and was launched in 1989.

👉🏾He also played a pivotal organisational , technical, and polotical role in the India Pokhram=|| nuclear test in 1989. the first since the original nuclear test by India 1974.

👉🏾From 1992 to 1997 Kalam was scientific advisor to the defense minister , and he later served as principal scientific adviser(1999-2001) to the government with the rank of cabinet minister.

👉🏾Kalam actively promoted education, particularly in the fields of science and technology, and he encouraged students to pursue their dreams.

👉🏾He authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds," which became popular among readers of all ages

👉🏾After his presidency, Kalam continued to engage in academic and research work and mentored numerous students and scholars.

👉🏾He was a strong advocate for sustainable development and renewable energy sources. Kalam was deeply concerned about the welfare of the underprivileged and consistently emphasized the importance of inclusive growth.

👉🏾Throughout his life, Kalam remained committed to promoting peace, harmony, and unity among diverse communities in India.On July 27, 2015, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam passed away while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, leaving behind a legacy of scientific innovation, leadership, and inspiration for generations to come.
